A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The People's President

1931-2015

Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was an illustrious Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he emerged from humble beginnings to become one of the most respected figures in India. His life and work continue to inspire millions across the globe.

Kalam's early life was marked by struggle and perseverance. He hailed from a modest family; his father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque, and his mother was a housewife. Despite financial constraints, Kalam's parents were determined to provide their children with a good education. Kalam attended Schwartz Higher Secondary School and later went on to attend St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, from where he graduated in physics. He then moved to Madras (now Chennai) to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).

His career began in the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he played a crucial role in developing India's first satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-Earth orbit in July 1980. Tribute

'Visionary Leadership'

Kalam had a clear vision for India’s future, particularly in the fields of science and technology. He aspired for India to be a developed nation and worked tirelessly towards this goal, including advocating for technological advancements and national development.

'Scientific Excellence'

Renowned for his work in aerospace engineering and defense technology, Kalam was instrumental in developing India’s satellite launch vehicle and missile programs. His contributions earned him the title "Missile Man of India."

'Humility and Simplicity'

Despite his high achievements and status, Kalam was known for his humble background and simple lifestyle. He was approachable, never shying away from interacting with common people, especially students.

'Inspirational Speaker'

Kalam had a unique ability to inspire and motivate people through his speeches and writings. His public talks were often filled with wisdom and encouragement, aimed at fostering a sense of responsibility and ambition in the youth.
